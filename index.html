About J.cole

Jermaine Lamarr Cole, also known as his stage name J. Cole, was born on January 28, 1985. Cole is a rapper, songwriter, and record producer. He is signed with record labels Dreamville, Roc Nation, and Columbia. Cole is from a city in North Carolina called Fayetteville.

Coles dream was to further pursue a solo career as a rapper. At a young age while attending highschool he knew that he just had to get out his little city to move on to better things. He made a brave decision to move to New York City. In hopes of caching his dream while attending school at St. John's University.

Cole got lots of his inspiration by Tupac, Nas, and Eminem. That helped him to then later produce an entire CD's worth of instrumentals. He then later traveled to Roc the Mic Studio in hopes of playing it for Jay-Z, but unfortunately was dissed by Jay-Z after having waited 3 hours in the rain for Jay-Z to arrive.

Jay Z rejection taught Cole a lesson, but he wasn't just done there. Having gotten the cold shoulder just brought Cole much more motivation to him. Cole kept working on his productions and later in 2009 he released his second mixtape The Warm Up. It became a big hit all over the radio. Now J.Cole has millions of fans from all over the world.
